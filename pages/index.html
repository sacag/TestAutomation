About Microsoft

Microsoft Corporation is an American multinational technology corporation headquartered in Redmond, Washington. Founded on April 4, 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen, Microsoft grew from a small software startup into one of the world's most valuable companies, transforming how people work, communicate, and create.

Company Facts

Founded: April 4, 1975

Headquarters: Redmond, Washington, USA

CEO: Satya Nadella (since February 2014)

Employees: Over 220,000 worldwide as of 2024

Stock: MSFT (NASDAQ)

Key Products and Services

Microsoft's portfolio spans operating systems, productivity software, cloud infrastructure, gaming, and artificial intelligence. Windows remains the world's dominant desktop operating system, while Microsoft Office and Microsoft 365 power productivity for hundreds of millions of users. Azure is the second-largest cloud platform globally, competing directly with Amazon Web Services and Google Cloud.

The company also owns LinkedIn, the world's largest professional network, and GitHub, the leading platform for software development collaboration. Microsoft's Xbox division makes it a major player in interactive entertainment.

Leadership and Culture

Under CEO Satya Nadella, Microsoft adopted a growth mindset culture and pivoted to a cloud-first, mobile-first strategy. Nadella's leadership transformed the company's reputation from a closed, combative corporation to an open, collaborative technology leader.

Artificial Intelligence

Microsoft has made significant investments in artificial intelligence, partnering with OpenAI and integrating AI capabilities across its entire product suite under the Copilot brand. These AI features span Office, Azure, GitHub, Bing, and Windows.

Financial Highlights

Microsoft consistently ranks among the world's most valuable companies by market capitalisation, regularly competing with Apple and NVIDIA for the top position. Its fiscal year 2024 revenue exceeded $245 billion, with the Intelligent Cloud segment (Azure and server products) being the fastest-growing division.

Investors can track performance and access earnings reports on the Microsoft Investor Relations page. The company trades on NASDAQ under the ticker symbol MSFT and has paid a dividend every year since 2003.
